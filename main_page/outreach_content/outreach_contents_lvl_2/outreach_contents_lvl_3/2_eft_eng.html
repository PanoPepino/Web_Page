Effective Field Theories

  • An effective field theory is a type of approximation for an underlying physical theory. The approximation is so that only relevant physical parameters are taken into account for the description.
  • This effective description is only valid at a specific range of energy scales. The smaller the distance at which one wants to describe some phenomena, the more energy is required. Beyond a given threshold of energy, the effective description is no longer accurate and another description that takes into account more relevant parameters is required.
  • The four fundamental forces of Nature are described using effective field theories.
  • Effective field theories at higher energy scales usually have a link to their lower energy counterparts. As an example, starting from Einstein's General Relativity (the green floating island), the parameter of curvature of the spacetime could be considered negligible. Doing this, one would recover Newton's description of gravity (the island at the forefront, where Einstein is going to pay a visit to Newton).
  • As an effective field theory, General Relativity description also fails at sub-atomic distances, where quantum effects should be taken into account. This requires a quantum description of gravity, i.e. a theory of quantum gravity.
  • String theory (the distant purple island) is the best candidate theory, not only to provide a quantum description of gravity, but to unite the four fundamental forces of Nature under the same formalism.
