The Swampland

  • The difficulty of obtaining a four-dimensional description compatible with observations from supergravity has motivated physicists to think in a different way.
  • Exploring the Landscape in search of scarce descriptions with positive value of the dark energy is no longer the choice. Instead, physicists now study what properties four-dimensional effective field theories should have such that they can be obtained from string theory.
  • The Swampland contains all four-dimensional effective field theories that can be created ad-hoc and seem to describe quantum gravity at low energies, but that cannot be obtained from string theory.
  • This region can be thought to be way bigger than the Landscape, populated with theories that pretend to offer a quantum gravity effective description, but in reality are monsters that could never be obtained from a true theory of quantum gravity, like String Theory.
  • The mathematical tools to understand this region are not completely developed yet. Hence, physicists cannot provide theorems about the Swampland, but conjectures. Some conjectures even claim that, maybe, positive values for the dark energy (Λ > 0) will never be obtained from string theory. What does this mean for string theory?
  • Other conjectures state that some type of solutions with negative dark energy (Λ < 0) are unstable and will decay through to even lower values of Λ by the emission of... "bubbles"?
